## FACTUAL NARRATIVE

The following is a summary of the prosecution case as disclosed. The Appellant's position on each step is addressed under the Grounds below.

On 9 October 2024, SC Deneka and SC Ferry were conducting patrols on CityLink Freeway. At approximately 09:24–09:25 AM, SC Deneka states he observed the Appellant's white BMW sedan (registration BQX110) travelling south and decided to intercept it "for a routine check." The vehicle was intercepted on the Dynon Road off-ramp, West Melbourne.

SC Deneka administered a preliminary breath test (PBT) — negative for alcohol. He then administered a preliminary oral fluid test (POFT) using a Securetec DrugWipe II Twin device. Based on the result of that test, he formed the opinion that the Appellant's oral fluid contained a prescribed illicit drug, and required the Appellant to accompany him to the police testing vehicle for an oral fluid test (SOFT/OFT) under s 55E RSA.

At 09:51 AM, the Appellant provided an oral fluid sample. The SOFT device, described in the intercept proforma as a "Securetec DrugWipe II Twin Combo," detected a prescribed illicit drug. At 11:52 AM, SC Deneka placed the retained portion of the sample in a locked refrigerator.

The sample (S0118696, in tamper-evident bag T300511170) was conveyed by VPS3 Samantha Lambert on 14 October 2024 and received by VIFM Toxicology at 08:32 AM on 14 October 2024. Analysis by approved analyst Mark Chu, dated 15 October 2024, confirmed methamphetamine.

A s 84(1) RSA certificate (Jacinta Thomson, VicRoads, dated 4 December 2024) was obtained in relation to the Appellant's licence status.

A brief of evidence was prepared and served. Multiple disclosure requests were made by the Appellant between May and November 2025. A court-ordered disclosure deadline of 15 September 2025 was set by Magistrate Hartnett on 29 July 2025. The full brief was not completed until November 2025, approximately 63 days after the ordered deadline.

## GROUNDS OF APPEAL — CONVICTION

The appeal is a full rehearing before the County Court. The prosecution must prove each element of each charge beyond reasonable doubt on the evidence led at the appeal hearing. The following grounds identify where the prosecution case, on the disclosed evidence, fails to meet that standard or is subject to exclusion.

### GROUND 1 — POFT PRECONDITION FAILURE (s 55D RSA): COUNTS 2 AND 3

**The statutory gateway:** RSA s 55D(1) requires that an authorised officer *form the opinion* that the result of the POFT *indicates* the presence of a prescribed illicit drug. That formation of opinion is the mandatory precondition to any requirement under s 55E. Without a lawful s 55D opinion, no requirement to provide a sample under s 55E is enlivened, and all downstream evidence (the SOFT result, the VIFM analysis, and the s 57B certificates) is obtained without lawful foundation.

**The device reading rule:** The Securetec DrugWipe II Twin manufacturer's instructions (disclosed by Deneka, 31 October 2025) state in terms:
- *Negative:* result is negative when the red control line appears and **no additional red test lines** develop.
- *Positive:* result is positive when the red control line **and** the red test line develop.

**The evidentiary gap:** SC Deneka's BWC (file: Axon Body 3 Video 2024-10-09 0932 X60J0288H) did not begin recording until **09:32 AM** — approximately 7–8 minutes after the intercept and POFT administration commenced. The POFT was conducted in the period 09:24–09:32 during which Deneka's own BWC was not recording.

SC Ferry's BWC (file: Axon Body 3 Video 2024-10-09 0925 X60J0491H), which commenced at 09:25 and would have covered the POFT, has been **visually redacted from 09:24:37 to 10:15:32** — covering the entire relevant period. The basis for that redaction ("law enforcement data security standards") is addressed separately under Ground 4.

**The result:** There is no objective video record of the DrugWipe device readout. The prosecution relies entirely on SC Deneka's lay opinion ("in my opinion, the result of the preliminary oral fluid test indicates..."). That opinion cannot substitute for the objective device result that the statute requires. SC Collins (Melbourne Prosecutions, 2 June 2025) confirmed in writing that conclusory statements in the brief would be supported "by the informant's viva voce evidence or body worn camera footage where applicable" — an acknowledgment that the objective record was not in the brief itself.

At the contest mention on 6 January 2026, SC Batth (prosecution) conceded on the record: *"there's no such document"* in response to inquiries about standard operating procedures for the POFT device.

**Consequence:** Without lawful s 55D enlivenment, the s 55E requirement to provide a sample was ultra vires. Evidence obtained in consequence is subject to exclusion under s 138 *Evidence Act 2008* (Vic). The s 49(4) RSA evidentiary presumption flowing from the s 57B certificates collapses if the certificate chain is founded on an unlawful procedure.

**Authorities:** *Evidence Act 2008* (Vic) ss 76, 79, 135, 138; *Road Safety Act 1986* (Vic) ss 55D, 55E; Road Safety (General) Regulations 2019 (Vic) regs 15, 16.

---

### GROUND 2 — UNLICENSED DRIVING: FAILURE TO NEGATIVE THE EXEMPTION (COUNT 1)

**The offence:** RSA s 18(1)(a) makes it an offence to drive without being licensed under the Act. However, RSA s 18(1)(a) read with Road Safety Drivers Regulations 2019 (Vic) reg 12 and reg 13(1)(a) provides that a holder of a non-Victorian licence is *authorised* to drive in Victoria *unless* they have resided in Victoria for more than 6 months.

**The prosecution's burden:** The prosecution must prove beyond reasonable doubt that the Appellant was *not* within the reg 12 exemption — that is, that he had resided in Victoria for more than 6 months at the date of the offence. The s 84(1) certificate (Jacinta Thomson) certifies only that the Appellant was not licensed under the RSA. It says nothing about duration of residence. It cannot fill the residency gap.

**The evidentiary position:** SC Deneka's statement (para 15) asserts: *"GARRARD was not exempted or otherwise authorised to drive on his Queensland Drivers Licence, having lived in Victoria for a period exceeding 6 months at the time."* This is a bare conclusory assertion by the informant on a mixed question of fact and law. No documentary evidence of residence duration — tenancy records, utility accounts, registration documents, or equivalent — is contained anywhere in the brief.

**The BWC contradiction:** SC Deneka's own BWC footage at timestamp 09:49 AM shows the officers confirming to the Appellant that his licence was "all good." This directly contradicts the charge. The prosecution must reconcile this concession on the record with the subsequent charge.

**Consequence:** On the disclosed evidence, the prosecution cannot prove beyond reasonable doubt that the Appellant was outside the reg 12/13 exemption. The charge under s 18(1)(a) is not made out.

**Authorities:** *Road Safety Act 1986* (Vic) s 18(1)(a); *Road Safety Drivers Regulations 2019* (Vic) regs 12, 13(1)(a); *Evidence Act 2008* (Vic) s 141.

---

### GROUND 3 — CHAIN OF CUSTODY GAP: COUNT 3

**The gap:** The oral fluid sample (S0118696) was placed in a locked refrigerator by SC Deneka at **11:52 AM on 9 October 2024**. The Lambert continuity statement records that Lambert collected the sample from the Road Policing Drug and Alcohol Section and conveyed it to VIFM Toxicology, where it was received at **08:32 AM on 14 October 2024**.

This creates an unaccounted gap of approximately **4 days and 20 hours** between the sample being placed in the refrigerator and Lambert's collection. No document accounts for who had access to, or custody of, the sample during that interval. The continuity notes section of the intercept proforma (page CD 9) is entirely blank — the fields for the name of the authorised officer, date/time, and name of the person are unfilled.

**The Lambert statement:** The continuity statement was signed by Samantha Lambert on **8 October 2025** — approximately **363 days** after the events it purports to record — with no reference to any contemporaneous record. The statement itself was prepared on 7 October 2025.

**Consequence:** The prosecution cannot establish beyond reasonable doubt that the sample received by VIFM and analysed by Mark Chu is the same sample taken from the Appellant. Without that authentication, the s 57B(4) certificate and the s 49(4) presumption cannot operate.

**Authorities:** *Evidence Act 2008* (Vic) ss 55, 135, 137; *Road Safety Act 1986* (Vic) s 57B(4).

---

### GROUND 4 — DEFECTIVE s 41A DISCLOSURE CERTIFICATE AND IMPROPER REDACTION

**The redaction:** Ferry's BWC footage (09:24:37–10:15:32) has been substantially redacted. The Schedule 1 to the s 41A certificate (Deneka, 17 November 2025, reviewed by Sgt Bowen 35440) lists the redacted material and gives the following basis: *"These were redacted in accordance with law enforcement data security standards."*

**The statutory basis for withholding:** CPA s 41A(1)(a) permits the informant to withhold material only on three grounds: (i) a court-determined claim of privilege or public interest immunity; (ii) an immunity conferred by statute; or (iii) a relevant statutory prohibition or restriction. "Law enforcement data security standards" is none of these. No court has determined any PII claim. No statutory immunity or prohibition is identified.

**Self-authorisation:** Deneka's email of 31 October 2025 states in terms: *"I authorised the redactions."* The informant unilaterally withheld the objective video record of the POFT procedure without any court order, DPP determination, or s 45 CPA non-disclosure certificate.

**What was withheld:** The redacted period 09:24:37–10:15:32 in Ferry's footage encompasses the entirety of the roadside interaction including the POFT administration. This is the only BWC footage that would have captured the DrugWipe device readout objectively. Its withholding prevents the Appellant from testing the s 55D foundation.

**Consequence:** The s 41A certificate is defective. The court should draw adverse inferences from the withholding of the objective record: *Jones v Dunkel* (1959) 101 CLR 298. The improperly obtained and withheld evidence engages s 138 *Evidence Act 2008*.

---

### GROUND 5 — AUTHORISED OFFICER AUTHORITY VARIANCE: COUNTS 2 AND 3

SC Deneka's statement (para 8) describes his authority as being *"for the purpose of carrying out the procedure for the provision of a sample of oral fluid under section 55E(6)."* The authority card produced as an exhibit uses the word "PROVISION." However, RSA s 55E(6) requires that the officer be authorised to *take* a sample; s 57B(3) refers to the certificate as the "Certificate of the Taking of an Oral Fluid Sample." If the authority granted is limited to "provision" by the person tested rather than the act of "taking" by the officer, the s 55E(6) precondition may not be satisfied.

## GROUNDS OF APPEAL — SENTENCE

In the event the Appellant is convicted on any charge, the sentence is manifestly excessive and fails to give proper weight to:

1. The Appellant's presence in Victoria was compelled by compliance with a County Court Community Correction Order, not voluntary travel.
2. The Queensland driver licence was valid and current at the time of the intercept.
3. Disqualification periods of 18 months and 6 months consecutive impose disproportionate hardship on a person whose interstate licence was operative and who was present in Victoria for compliance purposes.
4. The aggregate fine of $2,800 does not appear to reflect parity with comparable matters.
